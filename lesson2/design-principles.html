Design Principles

Stephanie Clark

Visual Hierarchy

Apple

https://www.apple.com/

When the page first loads, you see the iPhone 13 Pro, their most recent iPhone for now. As you scroll down you see the iPhone SE and then you start to see other products that are smaller than the main two. I believe this shows what Apple is not only trying to sell, but they may be trying to guess what the user wants to see.

Fitt's Law

Target

https://www.target.com/

What I love about Target's mobile site is how big the clickable space is. For each product it probably gets a fourth of the screen size and they are mostly in columns of two. Even when you click on the menu, each option is easy enough and close enough together to tap. All of the sign in or sign up buttons are noticeable because of their size and easy to get to.

White Space and Clean Design

PayPal

https://www.paypal.com/us/home

PayPal’s home page is clean. There isn’t a ton there and each section is spaced enough apart from the one before and after that you can see the separation. The white space they give not only makes it easier to read, but makes the design more pleasing to the eye. There isn’t a ton to focus on. It feels organized and not chaotic.
